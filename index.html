The scale of it

Dental wear leaches plastic.

Dental devices break down with wear, shedding microplastics that build up in the body over time. The two that touch you most:

RetainerWorn nightly for yearsPET-G thermoplasticMicro- and nanoplastics, shed through years of abrasion and swallowed in saliva

The risks

What microplastics do to your body

Lungs

Airborne particles found deep in lung tissue, linked to airway inflammation.

Gut

Swallowed daily and recovered routinely from the gut; linked to inflammation.

Hormones

BPA and plasticisers shed during wear are recognised endocrine disruptors.

Pregnancy

Found in placental tissue; children and the unborn are most at risk.

Source: Shariff et al. (2025), Progress in Orthodontics 26(1), Article 49.
